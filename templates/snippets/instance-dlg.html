BIBFRAME Instances

A BIBFRAME Instance reflects an individual, material embodiment of a BIBFRAME Work and is conceptually similar to a FRBR Manifestation. An Instance can have specific subclasses to further distinguish specific types of Instances.

  • Archival

    Resources created, accumulated, and used by different BIBFRAME Agents while conducting their normal activities that are preserved because of their continued value.

  • Electronic

    An Electronic Instance is a resource that is accessed or manipulated through a computing device and can reside either on the local device or on a remote server.

  • Manuscript

    A Manuscript Instance is a resource that is handwritten or typed and are generally unique.

  • Monograph

    A Monograph Instance is a single unit cataloging resource, commonly know as a book.

  • Serial

    A Serial Instance is a multiple unit cataloging resource issued in successive part without an definitive end.
